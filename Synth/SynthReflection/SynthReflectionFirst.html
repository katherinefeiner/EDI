The steps were as follows:

  1. Gathered materials (5 K Ω potentiometer, 2x 1M Ωpotentiometer, 1K Ω resistor, 0.01μF ceramic capacitor, 0.1 μF ceramic capacitor, 10 μF electrolytic capacitor, switch, 8Ω K speaker, 9V battery, 556 chip, chip socket, perf board, bread board, battery clip)
  2. Cut and stripped 6 yellow wires, 2 black wires, and 2 red wires with 2.2 width and ~3 to 4 inches in length
  3. Sautered yellow wires onto 3 potentiometers (two 1M Ω and one 5K Ω)
  4. Followed schematic and attached all my pieces to my breadboard
  5. Tested the accuracy of my breadboard using a multimeter (no sound when one probe in + side and one probe in - side = good to go)
  6. Attached the speaker and battery
  7. Troubleshooted when no volume came out → turns out my speaker wasn’t working so I replaced the speaker
  8. Turned knobs of my 3 potentiometers and heard a sound!
